Intel’s Sustainability Journey

From Foundation to Future: How Intel Changed the World

1968

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, planting the seed for the modern computing age.

1971

Released the Intel 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor—revolutionizing electronics and computation.

1981

Powered IBM’s first personal computer with Intel’s x86 processor, ushering in the PC era.

1990s

Intel’s processors enabled explosive internet growth, laying the hardware foundation for the World Wide Web.

2000s

Introduced energy-efficient chip designs (e.g., Intel Core), balancing performance and power for a greener world.

2016

Expanded into AI and autonomous driving with acquisitions like Mobileye, reshaping how tech impacts daily life.

2020

Achieved 100% renewable energy use for all U.S. operations, setting an industry standard for sustainability.

2023

Opened Intel’s first net-zero carbon certified factory in Chandler, Arizona—paving the way for green manufacturing.

2040

Intel aims for net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across all global operations and equitable AI access worldwide.
